Scenes From A Window An experiential artist book that re-evaluates our relationship to nature in a global digital maelstrom.

generative design,
creative coding,
new media art

LASALLE College of the Arts
2023

CONCEPT   This is an experiential artist book that re-evaluates our relationship to nature in a global digital maelstrom. The recent pandemic-ridden years have led to a more intense engagement with technology.

By algorithmically appropriating screenshots on Google Street View, abstract sceneries, that are familiar to the subconscious, are created.

A parallel is drawn between images viewed through our device screens and real landscapes framed by windows. Thus, perhaps, even the simple experience of ‘the outside’ can start to be automated.

METHOD   In order to create the images, different cities around the world were selected and screenshots of their streets were taken on Google Street View, which were vaguely sorted according to colour and texture and placed in a grid.

Using p5.js, each individual pixel of each grid was isolated and subjected to a series of conditions pertaining to its red, blue or green value. If it passed, an ellipse was drawn at the rough location of the source pixel.

Thus, an abstract scenery is formed that is open to perception.


ARTIST BOOK   'This artist book consists of several hand made frames containing images within. It is bound with a band that can be removed so that the reader can dictate the order of the images according to their own narrative.

ARTIST BOOK   The frame perpetuates the idea of a ‘window’: both a digital, in the form of screens, and a real one, which frames landscapes.
